Ancient Stonehenge and Medieval Salisbury Salisbury Plain, Wiltshire, England. One of the planet's oldest World Heritage Sites, Stonehenge has been a place of pilgrimage for more than 4,500 years. It was believed to have been erected as a place of worship, but these days, the crowds consist of tourists drawn by the sheer scale of this magnificent monument to mankind's ingenuity. It's a sprawling site, covering an area of more than 20 square kilometers and boasting a state-of-the-art visitor center.
The Colosseum In the centre of the city of Rome, Italy,east of the Roman Forum. The most famous and largest structure still standing from the Roman Empire, the Colosseum is also the biggest attraction of modern-day Rome.In ancient days,the Colosseum was large enough for theatrical performances, festivals, circuses, or games, which the Imperial Court and high officials watched from the lowest level, aristocratic Roman families on the second, the populace on the third and fourth.
Pyramids of giza In the Giza Governorate, to the west of the Egyptian capital of Cairo. The Pyramids of Giza are the largest and most recognizable pyramid structures in the world. They were built to honor certain Pharaohs of the fourth ruling dynasty of Egypt during a period known as the Old Kingdom.Pyramids were the burial places for Egyptian royalty during the Old Kingdom. The three large pyramids at Giza were built for three generations of Egyptian kings: Khufu, his son Khafre, and his grandson Menkaure.
The Great Wall of China From west to east in northern China,near Beijing It took more than 2,500 years to build the Great Wall, China's most recognizable symbol, which snakes through the northern part of the country for more than 13,000 miles. During the 8th century B.C.E., the Zhou dynasty-era state of Chu began construction on the wall to protect against foreign invaders. Most tourists explore only a section or two of the stone and brick fortification; it would take approximately 177 days of nonstop walking to see the entire wall.
